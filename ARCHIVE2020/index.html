UCR Programming Competition is a 5 hour programming competition open to all undergraduate students in UCR. Each team consists of 3 people and will be challenged to solve 10 programming problems.

UCRPC RULES

  1. Teams are capped at 3 members each
  2. You will be given 5 hours to solve a total of 10 problems
  3. You will NOT be allowed internet access, smart phones, watches, etc during the contest, but you are allowed printed materials, so textbooks and cheatsheets are allowed.
  4. The grading scale is an all or nothing system, where if you fail a single test case or your program does not finish in the allocated amount of time, you get no points. You are not given visibility of the test cases. Ranking will be based first on score, then on total submission time, which is offset by the number of failed attempts as a time penalty.
  5. You will be allowed a few of the standard languages (C++, C, Java, Python) but grading WILL NOT take that into account. Meaning a problem that expects you to finish within 3 seconds for the given inputs may not necessarily finish in time for a Python solution, despite a correct algorithm. Note that no libraries outside of their standard libraries will be provided.

FAQ

  1. What is a programming competition?

    2. A programming competition is a place where you and your team compete with other teams on a set of challenging programming problems.

  2. Who can participate?

    3. All undergraduate students in UCR are allowed to participate

  3. Is there free food?

    4. Yes. Lunch will be provided.

  4. Do I need a team?

    5. Teams are not required, but are recommended. Teams are capped at 3 members

  5. Is UCRPC free?

    6. Yes it is free to participate

  6. What should I bring?

    7. Please bring any printed materials that you will need to reference during the competition (e.g. Competitive Programming 3, Competitive Programmer's Handbook)

  7. What if I’m a freshmen?

    8. You are very welcome to particpate. We also have a prize for the best freshmen team.

  8. What if I still have questions?

    9. Please feel free to email Ji Hwan Kim at jkim393@ucr.edu
